# HSIL Governance Model  
Version 1.0 — Draft  
Created by: Khalid Majd (2025)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the governance structure for the Human Semantic Intention Language (HSIL). Its goals:

- preserve openness  
- ensure stability  
- maintain extensibility  
- guarantee predictable versioning  
- guide community contribution  

---

## 2. Steering Committee (HSC)

The HSC is the core governing body of HSIL.

### Responsibilities
- Approve new HSIL versions  
- Review and vote on HSIPs  
- Maintain backward compatibility  
- Publish specifications and release notes  
- Oversee reference implementations  

### Membership
- 5–12 voting members  
- Selected from active contributors  
- Expertise in AI, semantics, cognition, or agent systems  

### Founding Chair
**Khalid Majd**  
Permanent seat, permanent voting rights.

---

## 3. Working Groups

Specialized groups for:

- Emotional modeling  
- Intent graph architecture  
- Alignment and safety  
- Agent frameworks  
- Multi-agent semantics  
- Human-computer interaction  

Working Groups produce recommendations.  
HSC makes final decisions.

---

## 4. Decision Policy

### Minor Releases (0.x → 0.x+1)
Simple majority of HSC.

### Major Releases (0.x → 1.0 or 1.x → 2.0)
75% approval of HSC + Founding Chair approval.

### Patch Releases
Minimum 2 HSC approvals.

---

## 5. Compatibility Guarantees

From HSIL v1.0 onward:

- No breaking syntax changes  
- No removal of existing fields  
- Only additive expansions  
- Deprecation allowed but not deletion  

---

## 6. Proposal Workflow

All changes follow the HSIP system:

/proposals/HSIP-TEMPLATE.md


---

## 7. Neutrality

HSIL is:

- vendor-neutral  
- platform-neutral  
- royalty-free  
- openly licensed  

No company may claim exclusive control.

---

## 8. Amendments

Changes to this document require:

- 75% HSC approval  
- Founding Chair approval
